My Love Letter to Kombucha
(a.k.a. the Drink with a Tiny Science Lab Inside)

If coffee is the friend who wakes you up with an air horn, kombucha is the quirky friend who arrives wearing mismatched socks, brings a jar full of microorganisms, and somehow makes your day better.
For anyone unfamiliar, kombucha is a fermented tea made by feeding sweet tea to a SCOBY (Symbiotic Culture of Bacteria and Yeast).

The microbes happily munch through the sugar, producing organic acids, carbonation, and countless tiny bubbles that dance around the bottle like they're celebrating their own microscopic party. The result? A refreshing, tangy drink that many people enjoy for its probiotics, antioxidants, and gut-friendly reputation.

I've been brewing my own kombucha for over a year now, and it has become one of my favourite hobbies. Every batch feels like a tiny science experiment. One week it's delicately fizzy, the next it's practically auditioning to replace a champagne cork. Opening a bottle can sometimes feel like defusing a carbonated volcano—you never quite know whether you'll get a gentle psssht or an enthusiastic fountain decorating your kitchen ceiling. Fortunately, those surprise eruptions are just part of the fermentation adventure (and a reminder to respect the bubbles!).

My favorite kombucha flavors

  • Pineapple & Mint
  • Lemon & Black pepper
  • Watermelon & Apple

The best part is that no two batches are exactly alike. Different teas, fruits, herbs, and fermentation times create entirely different personalities. It's a drink that's alive, constantly changing, and always a little unpredictable—which is probably why I never get bored of making it.
